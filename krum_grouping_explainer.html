Krum selects one gradient, not averages. No noise cancellation.
The problem — gradient noise at scale
FedAvg: average 500 gradients
noise cancels out → clean signal
vs
Krum: pick 1 of 500 gradients
picks one noisy sample → noisy signal
Each worker: 32 samples → high variance gradient. FedAvg averages out the noise. Krum does not.
The fix — grouping reduces noise before selection
500 workers
noisy gradients
→
Group (k=10)
50 group averages
noise reduced ~3×
→
Krum selects
1 of 50 clean grads
much better pick
→
66%
vs 45% ungrouped
Under attack — grouped Krum gets both noise reduction and Byzantine filtering
FedAvg (no filtering)
Averages all 500 including 50 poisoned
Poison diluted but never removed
Ungrouped: 51.69%
Grouped: 61.83%
Krum grouped (filter + denoise)
Groups average out noise
Krum rejects poisoned groups entirely
Ungrouped: 44.92%
Grouped: 69.44%
Implication for the paper
Original hypothesis
"Grouping breaks existing defences"
Actual finding
"At scale (500 workers), selection-based defences need grouping. Grouping provides noise reduction that makes robust selection effective."
